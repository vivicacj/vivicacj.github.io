  • The 80/20 Rule is Real: Approximately 80% of your final score comes from just 4 Core Topics. If you are aiming for an A, the vast majority of your time and energy must be invested in deeply understanding and practicing these four modules.

Part 2: How to Learn ‚Äì Your Phased Study Path

Based on the data, you should adopt a three-phase study path: "Secure the Foundation First, then Conquer the Core, and Finally, Build Speed."

1

Phase 1: The Satellite Sweep

Goal: To quickly master the 6 satellite topics, build confidence, and ensure you make zero errors on these foundational questions.

Execution Sequence (The most efficient order):
  1. Day 1: "Indices & Standard Form" and "Inequalities".
  2. Day 2: "Ratio & Percentage" and "Statistics & Probability".
  3. Day 3: "Coordinate Geometry" and "Mensuration".
How to study:

Focus on the M-Layer (Memorisation) and P-Layer (Procedure). Write down every relevant formula three times. Then, use the practice sets for timed drills to be both fast and accurate.

2

Phase 2: The Core Topic Deep Dive

Goal: To truly understand the problem-solving models (SOPs) for the four Core Topics and be able to solve them independently.

Execution Sequence:
  1. Algebraic Manipulation: Master it first to build muscle memory.
  2. Quadratic Functions: Connect algebra with graph features.
  3. Geometric Proofs: Train your logical thinking and formal writing.
  4. Trigonometry Application: The final test of all your skills.
How to study:

For each Core Topic, strictly follow this process: Learn the SOP ‚Üí Imitate the Model ‚Üí Self-Correct using your mistake log.

3

Phase 3: Simulation & Skill Transfer

Goal: To break the boundaries between topics and improve your performance under real exam pressure.

Action:
  1. Full Exam Simulation: Once a week, take a complete 2022 exam paper under timed conditions.
  2. Advanced Variation Training: Complete "Variation Drill Packages" to challenge your thinking.
  3. Self-Assess with Radar Chart: After each mock, update your personal competency radar chart and return to Phase 2 for targeted reinforcement.
