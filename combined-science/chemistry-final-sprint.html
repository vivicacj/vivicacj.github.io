üß¨ AKM #202: Salt Preparation Methods
üìç Used in: Sprint 1 | Syllabus: 7.1(e-g) | Priority: ‚òÖ‚òÖ‚òÖ Critical
‚ñº
1Ô∏è‚É£ Academic Core Concept

üß† Conceptual Framework: Salt preparation is governed by the solubility principle. The method chosen depends on whether the salt and reactants are soluble or insoluble in water.

The Three-Path Decision Tree:

  1. Precipitation: For preparing insoluble salts from two soluble reactants.
  2. Titration: For preparing soluble salts from two soluble reactants (alkali + acid).
  3. Excess Reactant Method: For preparing soluble salts from a soluble acid and an insoluble reactant (metal, metal oxide, metal carbonate).
2Ô∏è‚É£ Exam Answer Toolkit

üìã Method Selection SOP:

STEP 1: Identify if the salt is soluble or insoluble (use solubility rules)

STEP 2: If insoluble ‚Üí Use Precipitation

STEP 3: If soluble, check reactants:

  • Both soluble (alkali + acid) ‚Üí Titration
  • One insoluble ‚Üí Excess Reactant Method

‚úçÔ∏è Excess Reactant Method - Complete Procedure:

  1. Add excess insoluble reactant to warm dilute acid in a beaker.
  2. Stir until the reaction stops (no more fizzing/no more solid dissolves).
  3. Filter the mixture to remove excess unreacted solid.
  4. Collect the filtrate and heat gently to evaporate until the solution becomes saturated (crystals start to form at the edge).
  5. Cool the solution to allow crystals to form.
  6. Filter to collect the crystals.
  7. Wash the crystals with cold distilled water.
  8. Dry the crystals between filter papers or in a warm oven.

üéØ Key Scoring Phrases:

  • "Why add excess?" ‚Üí "To ensure all the acid is completely neutralized/reacted, so no acid remains in the final salt."
  • "Why filter?" ‚Üí "To remove the excess unreacted solid from the salt solution."
  • "Why wash with cold water?" ‚Üí "To remove impurities on the surface of the crystals without dissolving them."
  • "Why heat until saturated?" ‚Üí "To concentrate the solution so crystals can form upon cooling."
3Ô∏è‚É£ Common Errors & Pitfalls

‚ùå ERROR 1: Saying "add excess to ensure complete reaction" without specifying what reacts completely.

‚úÖ CORRECTION: "Add excess to ensure all the acid is completely reacted."

‚ùå ERROR 2: Confusing which substance is in excess (student adds excess acid).

‚úÖ CORRECTION: Always add excess insoluble reactant (metal/metal oxide/metal carbonate), not acid.

‚ùå ERROR 3: Saying "wash with hot water."

‚úÖ CORRECTION: Wash with cold distilled water to minimize dissolving the salt crystals.
