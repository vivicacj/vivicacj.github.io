Stoichiometry (Limiting Reactant)
‚ñº

üìã Full Question:

üí° Why this question? This is the quintessential Limiting Reactant problem. It forces you to execute the full SOP: calculate moles of both reactants, compare them using the mole ratio, identify the L.R., and then use the L.R. for the final product calculation.

Answer Template:

(a) Calculate the number of moles of iron(II) carbonate...

(c)(i) Identify the limiting reactant...

Step 2 (Compare): To react with 0.0200 mol of FeCO‚ÇÉ, moles of HCl needed = 2 √ó 0.0200 = 0.0400 mol

Step 3 (Judge): We only have 0.00500 mol of HCl, which is less than the 0.0400 mol needed.

(c)(ii) Calculate the volume of carbon dioxide produced...

Step 2 (Moles of Product): Moles of CO‚ÇÇ produced = Moles of HCl / 2 = 0.00500 / 2 = 0.00250 mol
