Clinic Steps

Mindset Reset

Before touching the ruler

Dennis, drawing is just a connect-the-dots game with rules. Your algebra brain is powerful, but today it has to sit in the passenger seat. Do not use your calculator to escape the visual task.

  1. Translate the story into directions. A charged pith ball hangs on a string and is repelled horizontally by a charged plate. That means one vertical force, one horizontal force, and one diagonal force.
  2. Commit to the drawing command. If the question says "draw to determine", formulas are not the method. The ruler is the method.
  3. Use the central dot as your anchor. When your brain freezes, place one dot at the ball's centre. The dot tells every force where to begin.

FBD Step-By-Step

All arrows start at the dot

The 3-Force Rule for a hanging charged ball: Weight is strictly down, Electric Force is strictly horizontal away from the plate, and Tension points diagonally along the string toward the support.

  1. Draw one central dot. This dot represents the pith ball's centre of mass. Do not draw arrows from the string, plate, or surface.
  2. Draw Weight straight down. Use a ruler. The arrow must be vertical and labelled Weight or W.
  3. Draw Electric Force horizontally. The plate repels the charged ball, so the force is horizontal away from the plate. It does not slope.
  4. Draw Tension up-left. Tension follows the string back toward the support. It starts at the same central dot.

Vector Scale Diagram

Tip-to-tail, not tip-to-tip

NO CALCULATOR RULE: no Cosine Rule, no component calculation, no mental shortcut. Choose a scale, measure with a ruler, and let the triangle give the answer.

  1. Choose a scale. Example: 1 cm = 0.1 N. Write the scale before drawing, so every line has meaning.
  2. Draw Weight downward first. Start from a clean point. Use a ruler and draw the vertical downward vector to scale.
  3. From the tip of Weight, draw Electric Force. Your second vector starts at the arrowhead of the first vector. This is the tip-to-tail rule.
  4. Connect the start to the final tip. This gives the Resultant. To show equilibrium, close the triangle with Tension in the opposite direction.
